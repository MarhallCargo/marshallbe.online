1. Scope of this policy

This privacy policy describes how Marshall Relay Limited (referred to in this document as "Marshall Relay", "the company", "we", "our", or "us") handles personal information that we receive through our corporate website at marshallbe.online and through direct correspondence with the company.

This policy applies only to the corporate website and to communications addressed to the company. It does not apply to any of our consumer software products. Each of our products has its own product-specific privacy policy, which is presented to end users in the relevant app store listing and within the application itself.

3. Information we collect

We collect a limited amount of information through this website, falling into the following categories:

Information you provide directly

  • Contact information. When you write to us at any of our published email addresses, we receive the email address you write from, your name (if you choose to provide it), and the contents of your message.

Information collected automatically

  • Technical and usage information. When you visit our website, we and our analytics provider may collect standard log information such as your IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URL, pages viewed, and the date and time of your visit.
  • Cookies and similar technologies. See section 7 below.

We do not knowingly collect any special categories of personal data (for example, data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health data, or biometric data) through this website.

4. How we use information

We use the information described above for the following purposes:

  • To respond to enquiries and provide the information you have requested;
  • To operate, maintain, and improve our website;
  • To understand how visitors use our website in aggregate;
  • To detect, prevent, and respond to security incidents, fraud, and other unlawful activity;
  • To comply with legal, regulatory, and accounting obligations applicable to the company.

We do not use information collected through this website for advertising, profiling, or automated decision-making.

5. Legal basis for processing

Where the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or the United Kingdom GDPR applies, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Legitimate interests — for the operation, security, and improvement of our website, and to respond to your communications;
  • Consent — for any non-essential cookies and analytics, where required by applicable law;
  • Legal obligation — where we are required by law to retain or disclose information.

6. Sharing with third parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information. We share information only with the following limited categories of recipients, and only to the extent necessary:

  • Service providers. Reputable commercial vendors who provide hosting, analytics, email delivery, and similar infrastructure to support the operation of this website. These providers act on our instructions under written terms.
  • Professional advisers. Our corporate secretary, accountants, auditors, and legal counsel, where this is necessary for the lawful operation of the company.
  • Authorities. Government authorities, regulators, courts, and law enforcement, where we are legally required to disclose information, or where disclosure is necessary to protect our rights or the safety of others.

We do not transfer personal information to any third party for its own marketing purposes.

7. Cookies and analytics

This website uses a small number of cookies and similar technologies. We use them only for the following purposes:

  • Strictly necessary cookies required for the website to function correctly;
  • Analytics cookies, used to understand how visitors find and use our website. We currently use Google Analytics, a service provided by Google LLC, configured with IP anonymisation where supported.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of certain parts of the website. You can learn more about Google Analytics and your choices at policies.google.com/privacy.

8. Data retention

We retain personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this policy, or for longer where we are required to do so by applicable law (for example, for tax, accounting, or audit purposes).

  • Email correspondence: retained for the duration of the relationship and for a reasonable period thereafter, generally not exceeding seven years;
  • Server logs: retained for a limited period for security and operational purposes, then deleted or anonymised;
  • Analytics data: retained in accordance with the configuration of our analytics provider.
