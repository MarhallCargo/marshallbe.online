Protocol · Shadowsocks

Encrypted tunneling with modern ciphers.

Shadowsocks uses AEAD to protect the tunnel between your device and Marshall. Your data is encrypted in transit; we pair it on our own infrastructure with VLESS and Reality in TLS 1.3 — a stack focused on confidentiality, integrity, and a lean, auditable design.

AEAD protects your data in transit. On our servers it runs alongside VLESS, Reality, and TLS 1.3 — for confidentiality and integrity.

Per-packet authentication. No logs by design.

SOCKS5 transport with ChaCha20-Poly1305 and AES-256-GCM: each frame is cryptographically signed so replays and tampering are caught before your traffic is handled. The protocol keeps encryption and authentication in one place, which helps reduce metadata exposure and keep your side of the path private.

ChaCha20-Poly1305 / AES-GCM: per-packet auth, replays and tampering dropped at the edge. One place for crypto + integrity.

redundant encryption.

Traditional VPN protocols encrypt already-encrypted HTTPS traffic, wasting CPU and adding latency. VLESS with XTLS Vision detects TLS-in-TLS and passes the inner layer through directly — zero overhead on already-secure connections.
